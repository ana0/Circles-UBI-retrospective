# Circles UBI — accounting & inflation calculations

Reproducible backing for the Circles paper calculations: on-chain signup/supply
figures and the issuance-inflation (early-adopter advantage) model.

## Contents
- `inflation.py` — Alice/Bob balance tables under 7% issuance inflation (pure math, instant).
- `onchain_scan.py` — factory scan → weekly signup histogram → supply curve (issuance vs. incl. signup bonus).
- `hub_constants.py` — reads the Circles Hub monetary constants directly from chain (self-verifying; pure-python keccak).
- `results/signups_weekly.csv` — weekly signup counts (regenerated by the scan).
- `results/supply_over_time.csv` — modelled supply at each milestone: issuance alone and including the 50 CRC signup bonus.

`onchain_scan.py` reuses `results/signups_weekly.csv` if present (recomputes the supply
model instantly); pass `--rescan` to re-download ~2 years of logs from chain.

## Issuance model (Circles v1)
- Base issuance **8 tokens/day = 240/month**; one base-rate year = 12 × 240 = **2,880 tokens**.
- Rate steps **+7% per year** at the Circles `period` boundary (**365.2425 days** = 31,556,952 s).
- Rate is **global** (a function of system time, not signup time): in any given year
  every active user mints the same amount.
- Balances are **additive** — v1 has **no demurrage**; balances only ever grow.

|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| Month 1 | 83,600 | 10.37M | 4.18M | 14.55M | **40.3%** |
| Month 6 | 96,500 | 123.36M | 4.82M | 128.18M | 3.9% |
| Year 1 (365.24d) | 105,500 | 269.87M | 5.28M | 275.14M | 2.0% |
| Year 2 (730.5d) | 123,600 | 650.75M | 6.18M | 656.92M | 0.9% |

The signup bonus is a fixed, front-loaded lump (50 CRC × cumulative signups, capped
~123k × 50 ≈ 6.2M), so it is material against the small month-1 issuance base (~40%)
but negligible once issuance compounds into the hundreds of millions (<1% by year 2).
Full milestone list in `results/supply_over_time.csv`.

| End of year | Alice | Bob | Abs gap | Alice > Bob (z) |
|---|---:|---:|---:|---:|
| 2 | 5,962 | 3,082 | 2,880 | 93.5% |
| 3 | 9,259 | 6,379 | 2,880 | 45.1% |

| 10 | 39,791 | 36,911 | 2,880 | 7.8% |
| 20 | 118,067 | 115,187 | 2,880 | 2.5% |

Absolute gap is frozen at Alice's first-year issuance (2,880); relative gap decays
as `z(N) = 1 / (Σ_{n=1..N} 1.07^(n-1) − 1)`.

## Assumptions & caveats (read before citing)
1. **Accrued, not realized.** The supply curve is the upper bound assuming every
   signup mints continuously forever. Circles v1 only mints when a user calls
   `update()`; most inactive accounts never claimed their full entitlement, so
   *realized* circulating supply was substantially lower. (Quantifying that gap —
   summing mint `Transfer`-from-`0x0` events — is future work.)
2. **Month = 30 days; year = 365.2425 days** (the Circles period). The 7% step is
   applied at the year boundary measured from the factory creation time (the Hub's
   own deploy time differs by a few days — negligible at these totals).
3. **Signup bonus now reported separately.** Circles v1 minted a per-user signup
   bonus of exactly **50 CRC** (Hub `signupBonus`, read on-chain). It is included as
   its own column in `supply_over_time.csv` (`signup_bonus_50crc`) alongside issuance.
   Because it is a fixed front-loaded lump, it is material only early (~40% of the
   month-1 issuance figure) and <1% by year 2. Note it is arguably the *most* realized
   part of supply — minted atomically at signup, unlike accrued issuance which
   requires ongoing `update()` calls.
4. **Week-midpoint bucketing.** Signups are bucketed weekly and assumed to occur at
   the week midpoint (~0.5% effect on Month 1; smaller later).
5. **1:1 EUR peg context.** Circles held an informal 1 CRC ≈ €1 value until ~June
   2022 (~day 600, month ~20) — after the 7% step had already been active ~8 months.
